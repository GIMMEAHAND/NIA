Wholesale is the price you'd sell to a boutique, spa, or shop — they buy in bulk and mark it up themselves. Retail is what their customer (or yours) actually pays at checkout. Industry rule of thumb: wholesale runs about 2× your true cost, and retail runs about 2× wholesale — so a product on a store shelf is usually priced around 4× what it cost you to make.

Starting point suggested at 2× true cost — drag to test your own price.

Buying It All Separately Costs You More

Grabbing a cheap container here and a cheap sticker there feels like saving money in the moment, but it adds up against you: no bulk discount on any one thing, more shipping charges, and hours of your own time spent hand-labeling each one. Buying everything together, in bulk, from fewer places almost always works out cheaper once you count all of it honestly, the way this page just did.
